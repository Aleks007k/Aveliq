## What is each tool actually built for?

The two products serve fundamentally different buyers. Midjourney is a standalone image and video generation platform with subscription tiers designed for creators. OpenAI's image offering, GPT-Image-2, is an API-first product priced per token, built for developers embedding generation into applications. Comparing them directly is useful only if you're clear on which side of that line you sit.

## Is there a free tier?

No. Neither Midjourney nor OpenAI's GPT-Image-2 offers a free tier as of data collected June 2026. <!-- CHECK --> You pay before you generate, on both platforms.

## Which plan is cheapest?

That depends entirely on volume and use case. Midjourney sells subscriptions at four named tiers — Basic, Standard, Pro, and Mega — though specific monthly and annual prices are not available in the current data. <!-- CHECK --> A 20% discount applies to annual plans paid upfront.

OpenAI's GPT-Image-2 charges per token: $8.00 per 1M input tokens for images, $2.00 per 1M cached input tokens, and $30.00 per 1M output tokens. Text inputs run $5.00 per 1M tokens, with cached inputs at $1.25 per 1M. For low-to-moderate API volume, the Batch API saves 50% on inputs and outputs — useful if your workflow tolerates a 24-hour processing window.

## What do you get with a higher Midjourney plan?

The jump from Basic to Standard matters for one reason: Relax Mode. Standard, Pro, and Mega plans all include unlimited image generations under Relax Mode, which removes the anxiety of counting credits. Pro and Mega add unlimited video generations via Relax Mode as well.

Privacy is gated at Pro and above. Stealth Mode — keeping your images and videos private — is only available on Pro and Mega plans. <!-- CHECK --> For businesses, there's a harder requirement: companies generating more than $1,000,000 USD in gross annual revenue must be on a Pro or Mega plan to use Midjourney commercially.

## Who should use Midjourney?

Midjourney suits individual creators, design teams, and agencies who want a self-contained creative tool with predictable subscription costs. The unlimited generation model under Relax Mode makes it workable for high-volume visual production without per-image anxiety.

The revenue threshold rule is non-negotiable: if your business clears $1M in gross revenue, you're required to be on Pro or Mega regardless of actual generation volume. Stealth Mode, available only on those same tiers, adds private output for any team that can't afford public image indexing.

## Who should use OpenAI's GPT-Image-2?

GPT-Image-2 is the right call for developers and product teams who need image generation wired into an application or workflow. The token-based model scales linearly with usage — no subscription ceiling to hit, no Relax Mode queue to wait in.

The Batch API merits attention for teams with non-time-sensitive workloads. A 50% reduction on inputs and outputs is material at scale, and asynchronous processing over 24 hours is a reasonable trade for content pipelines, asset libraries, or overnight batch jobs. Pay-per-token also means costs track actual usage rather than a fixed seat, which suits teams with variable demand.

## Does either platform offer video?

Midjourney does. Unlimited video generation via Relax Mode is available on Pro and Mega plans. OpenAI's pricing data covers GPT-Image-2 for images only; no video generation pricing appears in the available data. <!-- CHECK -->

## Which one should you choose?

These tools are not substitutes for most buyers. They're parallel products aimed at different workflows, and the choice is structural rather than qualitative.

Choose Midjourney if you want a subscription-based creative tool, generate images at high volume, and don't need API integration. The commercial revenue threshold makes Pro or Mega the practical minimum for any business operating above $1M in gross revenue.

The more interesting question for the market is what happens as the two models converge. If Midjourney adds API access or OpenAI builds a richer consumer product, the clean segmentation above breaks down — and pricing pressure follows. Both companies have incentives to expand into each other's territory. Watch for it.
