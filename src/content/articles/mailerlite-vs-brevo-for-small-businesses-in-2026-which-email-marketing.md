Most small business owners picking an email platform are really asking one question: where does value stop and overpaying begin? MailerLite and Brevo both have free plans and legitimate paid tiers, but they're priced on different logic — one charges by subscriber count, the other by email volume. That difference shapes everything downstream.

## Which plan is cheapest for a new business with a small list?

Brevo's free tier costs nothing and sends up to 300 emails per day with no credit card required. That's a real starting point for a brand-new business that doesn't yet send in volume. MailerLite's free plan is also $0 and no credit card required, but caps at 250 active subscribers and 2,500 emails per month — more useful if your constraint is list size, not daily send frequency.

Neither free plan is identical, so "cheapest" depends on what you're bottlenecking against. A local business doing one weekly newsletter to a 200-person list fits inside MailerLite's free tier cleanly. A transactional or event-driven sender hitting daily sends fits Brevo's structure better.

## What does MailerLite actually give you?

MailerLite is built around a clean principle: pricing scales by subscriber count, not by which features you get. Every paid plan includes the full tool set. The Comfort plan starts at $12/month and includes multivariate testing, dynamic emails, campaign auto-resend, an AI writing assistant, and smart sending. Step up to Power at $25/month and you get unlimited monthly emails, unlimited user seats, unlimited automations, and 24/7 live chat support. Both paid tiers offer a 10% discount on annual billing, and nonprofits qualify for a 30% discount.

The free tier is generous for its size: campaigns, automations, all three email editors, one landing page, one website, and signup forms are all included. A 14-day trial of premium features is available with no credit card required. For a small business that wants to grow into its tool without hitting feature paywalls, that's the real pitch.

### Who should use MailerLite?

It suits businesses that are list-focused — a growing subscriber base is the primary asset, and you want the tool to grow alongside it without unlocking a new pricing wall every time you want a useful feature. Creators, newsletters, small e-commerce shops, and service businesses with defined audiences are natural fits.

## What does Brevo actually give you?

Brevo prices by email volume rather than contacts, which flips the model entirely. The Starter plan comes in at $9/month from 5,000 emails per month, making the entry point lower in dollar terms than MailerLite's paid tier. The Standard plan is $18/month. Jump to Professional and it's $499/month from 150,000 emails per month — a significant price step that signals this tier is not aimed at small businesses.

The feature set includes a drag-and-drop editor, A/B testing, an AI content generator, AI send time optimization, marketing automation with unlimited multi-step workflows, and contact scoring with RFM and CLV models. That last item is worth noting: RFM (recency, frequency, monetary) scoring is a serious CRM-adjacent capability, not a typical small business email feature. Brevo appears to be building toward a broader customer data platform rather than a pure email tool.

### Who should use Brevo?

Businesses that send high email volumes to a large contact base, or that want CRM-style segmentation baked into their email platform, will find Brevo's architecture more natural. The volume-based pricing rewards senders who email often rather than those who are simply growing a list.

## Is the pricing model the right fit for your business?

This is the question that actually decides it. If your list is small but you email it frequently, Brevo's volume model can be cheaper at the low end. If your list is growing fast but you send infrequently, MailerLite's subscriber-based pricing with no feature gating is likely the cleaner deal.

One real data gap: Brevo's data does not include yearly pricing, so it's not possible to compare annual billing across both platforms on equal terms. MailerLite publishes a 10% annual discount explicitly; Brevo's equivalent is not available from the source data.

## The verdict

For a small business that wants a full-featured tool without negotiating with a pricing wall, MailerLite's structure is more transparent and more forgiving as you scale. The free tier is functional, not a stripped teaser, and the Comfort plan at $12/month delivers features that competing platforms often reserve for higher tiers.

Brevo earns consideration if daily send cadence or volume is your constraint, or if you want RFM contact scoring without bolting on a separate CRM. The $9/month Starter plan is a genuine entry price. But the Professional plan's jump to $499/month means Brevo's pricing curve is steeper for businesses that outgrow the lower tiers.

When you start the free trial, test the specific workflow you'll actually run on day one — not the feature list, not the demo video. For MailerLite, that means building one automation and sending a campaign to your real list of 250 or fewer. For Brevo, set up a multi-step workflow and watch how the daily send cap interacts with your actual send schedule. The friction you find in the first hour is the friction you'll live with.
