The verdict up front: Otterly AI wins on price entry; Trakkr wins on coverage. What follows shows you exactly where each one breaks down — so you can match the tool to the actual job before committing budget.

AI search visibility is no longer optional for brands that care about how they appear in ChatGPT, Gemini, or Perplexity responses. The category is young enough that two credible platforms can look nearly identical on a features page while serving completely different buyers. Otterly AI and Trakkr are both built around prompt-based monitoring and citation tracking, but their architectures — one priced for lean teams, one built for agencies managing multiple brands — pull them toward different use cases fast.

## Which platform is cheapest?

Otterly AI's Lite plan runs $29/month, the lowest published price in this comparison. That gets you 15 search prompts per month. Trakkr's entry plan starts at $100/month for 1 brand, 3 team seats, and 50 prompts per brand across all 8 supported AI models.

The gap is real, but so is the context. At $29, Otterly gives you 15 prompts; at $100, Trakkr gives you 50 prompts plus multi-model coverage. What you're actually comparing is prompt volume and model breadth, not just a price tag.

## Is there a free tier?

Both products offer trials, but the structures differ. Otterly AI offers a free trial for new users — 14 days, no card required. Trakkr's trial is similarly defined: 14 days, full access to Growth features, no charge if you cancel before the trial ends.

Both windows are the same length. The practical difference is what you're testing: Otterly's base engine set versus Trakkr's full eight-model stack.

## What AI engines does each platform track?

This is where the gap widens materially. Trakkr explicitly lists 8 models on every paid plan: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Grok, DeepSeek, and Meta AI — all included from the $100/month Growth tier.

Otterly AI's base plans — $29, $189, and $489/month — include ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, and Copilot as the core four engines. Gemini and Google AI Mode are available as paid add-ons. Those add-ons are priced separately and can push the effective monthly cost well above the headline plan price; a fully loaded Otterly setup covering the same frontier models as Trakkr's base tier is meaningfully more expensive than the $29 or $189 entry point suggests.

For teams that need to monitor brand presence across the full range of frontier models, Trakkr's eight-engine baseline at $100/month is a structural advantage over Otterly's à la carte model expansion.

## Otterly AI: built for solo operators and budget-conscious teams

Otterly AI is straightforward. Three tiers — $29, $189, and $489 per month — scaled by prompt volume: 15, 100, and 400 prompts respectively. No hidden fees, no requirement for a separate OpenAI subscription, and you can cancel anytime. Add-on packages extend AI engine coverage up to 1,000 prompts.

The pitch is clean accessibility. A single-brand operator, a startup, or a consultant running one client's visibility tracking will find the Lite or Standard plan serviceable without a lengthy procurement conversation. The model is pay-for-prompts, not pay-for-seats, which keeps the math simple.

The risk is that "accessible" often means "limited." Base plans cover four engines; adding Gemini and Google AI Mode costs extra on top of the plan price. Fifteen prompts a month at the Lite tier leaves little room for competitive research alongside your own brand monitoring.

## Trakkr: built for agencies and multi-brand coverage

Trakkr's Scale plan supports 10 brands, unlimited team seats, 50 prompts per brand, and all 8 models. Monthly pricing is $500; the annual equivalent is €4,650, which the platform describes as a 17% saving. Note that the platform displays monthly prices in USD and annual prices in EUR — confirm the currency on the provider's site before purchasing, as the exact USD annual equivalent depends on the exchange rate at time of billing.

White-label support is included: custom domain branding at `ai.youragency.com`, plus client portal branding from an additional $49 per brand per month. API access opens up on the Scale tier and above. For agencies building AI search reporting into client deliverables, those features matter more than any per-seat price calculation.

The Growth plan's 3-seat limit is a genuine constraint for larger internal teams, and Trakkr's Enterprise pricing is not published — so high-volume teams will need to enter a sales conversation before they see a number.

## Which plan suits which buyer?

The decision tree is shorter than most vendors want you to think. If you're tracking one brand, working with a modest budget, and only need the four core engines, Otterly AI's $29 tier is the rational starting point. If you need Gemini, Google AI Mode, or the other frontier models on top of that, the add-on costs close the price gap with Trakkr faster than the headline numbers imply.

If you're running an agency with multiple clients, need white-label output, or require visibility across 8 AI engines from day one, Trakkr's Growth or Scale plan is the better structural fit. The one scenario neither product fully resolves in its published data is the mid-market brand team: more than a solo operator, fewer than a full agency roster, wanting broad model coverage without agency-tier pricing. That buyer should use the 14-day trial on Trakkr to test all 8 models on 10 to 15 actual customer-facing prompts — the fastest signal on whether the model breadth translates to coverage that actually matters for their brand.
