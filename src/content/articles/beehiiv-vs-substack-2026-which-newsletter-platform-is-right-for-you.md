Two models. One decision.

Beehiiv charges a flat monthly fee and takes nothing when your readers pay. Substack charges nothing upfront and takes 10% of every paid subscription forever. That single structural difference shapes everything else — which platform suits you depends almost entirely on where you are in your publishing journey and how fast you expect to grow revenue.

## Which platform is free to start?

Both are. Beehiiv's Launch plan costs $0, requires no credit card, and supports up to 2,500 subscribers with unlimited email sends, a newsletter website, and podcast tools. Substack's publishing tier is also $0 with no subscriber cap — you only pay fees once you turn on paid subscriptions.

The free tiers solve different problems. Beehiiv's cap at 2,500 subscribers means the free ride ends the moment you gain real traction. Substack lets you publish free indefinitely to any size list, which is a meaningful advantage for writers who are still building an audience and not yet charging for access.

Both platforms are built around a newsletter you charge for. If your goal is sending marketing campaigns to an existing customer list instead, [MailerLite and Brevo](/articles/mailerlite-vs-brevo-for-small-businesses-2026-which-email-marketing-to/) price that work very differently.

## What does each platform actually cost at scale?

Beehiiv's Scale plan runs $49 per month (or $517 billed annually) for up to 100,000 subscribers. The Max plan is $109 per month ($1,151 billed annually) for the same subscriber ceiling, adding features like removed beehiiv branding and higher AI credit limits — 50 per day versus 25 on Scale. An Enterprise tier exists for lists above 100,000 subscribers, billed on a flexible basis; beehiiv does not publish a fixed price for it.

Substack does not charge a monthly fee at any scale. Instead, it takes 10% of each paid transaction, on top of Stripe's 2.9% plus $0.30 per transaction, and a 0.7% billing fee on recurring subscriptions. Those fees compound as revenue grows. A creator generating substantial subscription income hands over a fixed percentage regardless of list size.

Platform fees are only half the story once money starts moving across borders. Where that revenue lands matters too — our comparison of [Payoneer and Revolut Business](/articles/payoneer-vs-revolut-business-for-freelancers-2026-which-pays-you-more/) covers what it costs to actually receive international payouts.

## Which platform has better monetization tools?

This is where the gap is real. Beehiiv's Scale plan and above include a 0% take rate on paid subscriptions, access to an Ad Network, and a Boosts Network — tools designed to generate revenue beyond reader subscriptions. Email automations also unlock at the Scale tier. For a creator who wants to monetize through advertising or referral incentives, not just subscriptions, beehiiv offers infrastructure that goes beyond what Substack's documented features cover.

Substack's monetization model is subscription-first. Creators set their own monthly, annual, and founding member prices — the founding member option lets readers pay any amount above the standard tiers. European readers can pay via iDEAL, Bancontact, Sofort, and direct debit, which reduces friction in markets where credit cards are less dominant.

## Who is beehiiv best for?

Beehiiv suits a creator who is already monetizing or has a clear plan to do so quickly. At $49 per month on the Scale plan, the flat fee becomes cost-efficient the moment subscription revenue would otherwise push Substack's 10% cut above that number. It also suits anyone who wants to run advertising alongside subscriptions — the Ad Network and Boosts Network have no equivalent in the Substack data provided.

The 2,500-subscriber free tier is tight. A fast-growing list will hit that ceiling in months, triggering the jump to $49 per month before a creator has necessarily validated paid demand.

## Who is Substack best for?

Substack is the stronger choice for writers who are early-stage, audience-first, and uncertain about when — or whether — they will charge for access. No subscriber cap on the free tier removes a forcing function that beehiiv imposes. You can build, experiment, and publish without a bill arriving.

The cost structure does penalize success. The 10% cut plus Stripe fees means that as paid subscriber revenue grows, the effective platform cost scales with it — unlike beehiiv's flat monthly rate. Writers who build a large, paying readership will eventually do the arithmetic and find the numbers favor a flat-fee model.

## The Verdict

**Start with Substack if** you are still building your list, not yet charging readers, and want zero fixed overhead while you find your audience. The unlimited free tier buys real runway.

**Switch to beehiiv** — or start there — once you have paying subscribers and a clear revenue trajectory. The 0% take rate on paid subscriptions at the Scale tier ($49/month) means you keep more of every dollar as revenue grows. Add the advertising and boost tools, and beehiiv is the more capable platform for creators who treat their newsletter as a business with multiple revenue lines.

The harder question neither pricing page answers: what does audience quality look like on each network, and does Substack's built-in discovery drive enough new subscribers to justify the 10% forever? That calculus is different for every writer — and it is the one worth running before you commit.
